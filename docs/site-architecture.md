# CHARM Site Architecture

This document defines the current website structure before development starts.

It is a planning doc, not a final design spec.

## Purpose

The website should feel:

1. clean
2. minimal
3. easy to scan
4. professional
5. soft and polished

The structure should feel familiar to property managers and condo decision-makers, while staying shorter and simpler than most competitor sites.

## Core Navigation

### Desktop Navbar

Left:

1. `CHARM`
2. small subtext: `Service de Nettoyage` or `Cleaning Services`

Center or right:

1. Home
2. Services
3. About
4. Contact

Far right:

1. `Request a Quote` button
2. `FR` language switcher

### Mobile Navbar

Top row:

1. `CHARM`
2. small subtext under or beside brand if space allows
3. hamburger menu

Mobile menu items:

1. Home
2. Services
3. About
4. Contact
5. Request a Quote
6. FR

### Navbar Behavior

1. Sticky on scroll
2. Soft glass or translucent surface is acceptable here
3. Keep it compact
4. The quote CTA should stay easy to find at all times

## Homepage Wireframe

The homepage should be short, clear, and conversion-focused.

### Section 1: Hero

Goal:
Explain who CHARM is, what CHARM does, where CHARM works, and what action to take next.

Content:

1. Brand: `CHARM`
2. Subtext: `Cleaning Services` / `Service de Nettoyage`
3. Headline
4. Short supporting paragraph
5. Primary CTA: `Request a Quote`
6. Secondary CTA: `View Services`
7. Short service-area note

Suggested message direction:

1. recurring cleaning for condo common areas
2. Saint-Laurent and nearby Montreal
3. clear communication and dependable service

Layout note:

1. Left-aligned copy
2. Right-side visual card or soft abstract panel
3. No photo required

### Section 2: What We Clean

Goal:
Let visitors confirm the service fit quickly.

Format:
Grid of simple cards

Suggested cards:

1. Lobbies
2. Hallways
3. Stairwells
4. Shared bathrooms
5. Garbage areas
6. Gyms
7. Recreational areas
8. Pool-side common areas

Layout note:

1. Clean icon or simple label
2. Keep the copy very short

### Section 3: Who We Work With

Goal:
Make the target audience obvious.

Suggested content:

1. Condo boards
2. Condo syndicates
3. Property managers
4. Building administrators

Why this matters:

1. It immediately tells the right people the site is for them
2. It helps filter out the wrong type of lead

### Section 4: Why CHARM

Goal:
Replace missing reviews/photos with operational trust.

Suggested content blocks:

1. recurring service focus
2. clear communication
3. shared-space cleaning specialization
4. dependable local service

Important note:
Because there are no reviews yet, this section should carry more trust weight in V1.

### Section 5: How It Works

Goal:
Make the quote/request flow feel easy.

Simple 3-step flow:

1. Tell us about your building
2. Get a quote or follow-up
3. Set up recurring cleaning

Layout note:

1. Horizontal on desktop
2. Stacked on mobile

### Section 6: Practical Info / FAQ

Goal:
Answer early objections without making the site feel crowded.

Suggested questions:

1. What areas do you clean?
2. What kinds of buildings do you work with?
3. Do you offer recurring cleaning?
4. Do you handle repairs or technical maintenance?
5. Can we contact you in English or French?

Note:
This can be a small FAQ block, not a full page at launch.

### Section 7: Final CTA

Goal:
Catch visitors who scroll to the end and are ready to act.

Content:

1. short CTA headline
2. 1-sentence reminder of service fit
3. quote/contact button

## Features

## Must-Have Features For V1

1. English-first layout
2. French route structure
3. Mobile-first responsive design
4. Sticky navbar
5. Quote/contact CTA on key pages
6. Structured contact form
7. Clean footer
8. Local SEO basics
9. Fast static pages

## Nice-To-Have Features For V1

1. FAQ accordion
2. subtle scroll reveal animation
3. soft glass effect on navbar or CTA cards
4. basic icon system

## Features To Avoid In V1

1. live chat
2. booking calendar
3. complicated search tools
4. fake review sliders with placeholder content
5. image-heavy hero layouts that depend on missing assets

## Copy Rules For The Whole Site

1. Keep headlines short
2. Keep paragraphs short
3. Prefer operational clarity over marketing language
4. Only include sections we can support with real information
5. Do not pretend to have reviews, awards, or credentials that are not confirmed
