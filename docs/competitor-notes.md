# Montreal Condo Cleaning Competitor Notes

Research date: 2026-04-28

## Category Clarification

The business should not be positioned as home cleaning or private residential cleaning.

The closest category is:

1. Condo-building common-area cleaning
2. Janitorial cleaning for multi-unit residential buildings
3. Cleaning for condo syndicates, property managers, and building administrators

Typical service scope in this category:

1. Lobbies
2. Hallways
3. Stairwells
4. Elevators
5. Shared washrooms
6. Garbage and recycling rooms
7. Gyms
8. Pool-side or recreational common areas

## Relevant Competitors

Website: <https://gabnetroyal.ca/entretien-immeubles-condos-montreal/>

Why it matters:

1. Very close to the target category
2. Explicitly targets condo syndicates, property managers, and building admins
3. Focuses on recurring common-area cleaning tasks

Takeaways:

1. Strong category fit
2. Practical and direct positioning

### Le Chiffon Dore

Website: <https://www.chiffondore.com/services/entretien-menager-coproprietes>

Why it matters:

1. Specifically targets coproprietes
2. Lists many of the same spaces this business wants to clean
3. Shows how service scope can be explained clearly

Takeaways:

1. The best content is concrete, not abstract
2. Shared-space lists help visitors self-qualify quickly

1. This is one of the closest comparison sites

## Visible Patterns In This Category

1. Most competitors are practical and quote-focused.
2. Many websites are text-heavy and visually generic.
3. Common-area task lists are more important than emotional lifestyle copy.
4. The target buyer is usually a manager, syndicate, or administrator.
5. Recurring schedules and service reliability matter more than flashy branding.

## Opportunity For CHARM

CHARM can stand out by being:

1. More focused on condo common-area cleaning only
2. Cleaner and easier to scan than generic janitorial sites
3. Bilingual
4. More direct about what is included and what is not included
5. Easy to contact for a quote

## Technical Notes

Based on public footers and markup, many local competitors appear to use agency-managed or builder-managed setups rather than fully custom code.

Examples:

1. Gabnet Royal credits Zone Graphique in the footer
2. Prestige Qualite credits Linkeo in the footer

This supports the case for a custom-coded website if long-term control matters.
