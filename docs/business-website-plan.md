# Condo Cleaning Website And Growth Plan

This document captures the current plan for building the family condo-building cleaning website, using it as a real portfolio case study, and turning the business into a practical income path while leaving room for future career changes.

## Current Situation

The family business is a cleaning service focused on condo-building common areas. Right now, the goal is not only to keep doing cleaning work, but to help the business become more independent, more professional online, and better at getting direct recurring contracts.

The website should become the first serious project in the portfolio. It should prove the ability to take a real small business, understand its needs, design a clear online presence, and support lead generation.

This can also become the foundation for freelance website work later. The first proof is the family cleaning business; after that, a few mockup sites can show the same skill applied to other business types.

## Personal Plan

1. Build the family cleaning business website first.
2. Use that website as the first real portfolio project.
3. Create a few polished mockup websites for other small businesses.
4. Market website services as a side hustle.
5. Keep the main focus on growing the cleaning business by improving the website, finding clients, and eventually helping organize workers.
6. Stay open to future career paths after university, including game design, UX/UI, web design, creative technology, or another job.

This plan is valuable even if the long-term career changes later. It builds transferable skills: design, user experience, communication, marketing, client work, project planning, and business development.

## Strategic Direction

The best positioning is not "I make websites for anyone." The stronger positioning is:

> I help local service businesses look professional online and get more quote requests.

For the family cleaning business, that means the website should help visitors answer five questions quickly:

1. Is this a real and trustworthy cleaning business?
2. Do they clean condo common areas like ours?
3. What areas are served?
4. How can I request a quote?
5. Can I communicate in English or French?

## Main Business Goal

The website should help the cleaning business get more direct clients, rather than relying only on subcontracted work, referrals, or informal contacts.

The site should support:

1. Direct quote requests from condo syndicates, property managers, and building administrators.
2. Clear service information for common-area cleaning.
3. Trust through reviews, honest business details, and clear scope.
4. Local search visibility for condo-building cleaning terms.
5. A professional first impression when someone looks up the business.

## Website Role

The website is not just a design project. It is part of a lead-generation system.

The full system should include:

1. Website
2. Google Business Profile
3. Review collection process
4. Work-quality photos of common areas when available
5. Clear quote/contact form
6. Simple social media presence
7. Outreach to potential clients
8. Follow-up process for inquiries

The website alone will not magically bring clients. It works best when paired with outreach, reviews, Google visibility, and consistent follow-up.

## Recommended Version 1 Website

Version 1 should be simple, trustworthy, bilingual, and focused on getting inquiries for recurring condo-building cleaning.

Recommended pages:

1. Home
2. Services
3. About
4. Contact / Request a Quote

Optional later pages:

1. Reviews
2. FAQ
3. Service-area pages
4. Scope or checklist page

## Design Direction

The site should feel:

1. Clean
2. Calm
3. Bright
4. Trustworthy
5. Local
6. Professional but not corporate
7. Minimal and sleek
8. Soft and pastel-leaning rather than loud

Avoid:

1. Overly flashy visuals
2. Generic stock-photo feeling
3. Empty marketing claims
4. Complicated animations
5. Pages that look nice but do not explain the service

Current design note:

1. Teal is the temporary main brand color.
2. The design can stay image-light for now because there is no logo, no photos, and no reviews yet.
3. The first visual system should rely on type, spacing, clean blocks, and a simple pastel-like palette.

## Technical Direction

The project should stay custom-coded so the business keeps full control. For this kind of site, a static-first framework is a better fit than a big app stack or raw vanilla HTML.

Recommended stack:

1. Astro
2. TypeScript
3. Tailwind CSS
4. Simple English/French route structure
5. Structured content files
6. Basic form handling

Keep the first version practical:

1. Fast loading
2. Mobile-first
3. Bilingual support
4. Clear contact form
5. SEO metadata
6. Simple content structure
7. Easy future updates

Content structure note:

1. Site structure should be informed by local condo-cleaning competitor sites.
2. We should follow familiar service-site content patterns when they help clarity.
3. We should only include sections we can support with real content.

Why this is a better fit than vanilla HTML/CSS/JS:

1. The site is bilingual, so duplicate pages will add up quickly.
2. Reusable layouts and components will make updates easier.
3. Static output keeps the site fast and simple.
4. The codebase stays fully portable and under control.

If custom code becomes too slow, a website builder is still acceptable. But because this is also a portfolio project, a custom-coded site is the stronger move.

The most important habit is follow-up. Many leads are lost because businesses answer slowly or vaguely.

## Simple Lead Process

When someone contacts the business:

1. Reply quickly.
2. Ask for location, building type, common areas to clean, frequency, and timing.
3. Confirm whether the job fits the service area.
4. Give a clear next step.
5. Record the lead in a spreadsheet.
6. Follow up if they do not respond.
7. After the cleaning, ask for a review if the client is happy.

Create a simple spreadsheet with:

1. Name
2. Contact info
3. Building or organisation
4. Area
5. Date contacted
6. Service needed
7. Frequency
8. Status
9. Quote amount
10. Outcome
11. Review requested
