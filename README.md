# CHARM Website

Website project for **CHARM SERVICE DE NETTOYAGE s.e.n.c.**, a residential and condo cleaning business serving Saint-Laurent and nearby Montreal areas.

The site is intended to be a simple, trustworthy, bilingual lead-generation website that helps the business win more direct clients instead of depending heavily on work from another company.

## Project Overview

This project will deliver a small business website focused on four things:

1. Clearly explain what the business offers.
2. Build trust quickly with local visitors.
3. Make it easy to request a quote or get in touch.
4. Support both English and French from day one.

This is not meant to be a flashy marketing site. It should feel clear, local, reliable, and easy to use on a phone.

## Assumptions

The following assumptions are guiding the initial plan and should be confirmed before launch:

1. Version 1 is a brochure-style lead generation website, not an online booking or payment system.
2. Primary contact methods will be phone, email, and a quote/contact form.
3. The main service area is Saint-Laurent, with additional nearby Montreal neighborhoods served when practical.
4. The site will have full English and French content, written or reviewed manually rather than auto-translated at runtime.
5. If the business does not have a public office, the site will emphasize service areas instead of publishing a full street address.
6. Reviews/testimonials will be available to publish with client permission.
7. Services are residential and condo-focused at launch. Commercial cleaning is out of scope unless confirmed later.
8. The public-facing brand can use `CHARM Service de Nettoyage`, while the footer and legal pages can use the full legal name `CHARM SERVICE DE NETTOYAGE s.e.n.c.`.

## Business Goals

1. Reduce dependence on referral or subcontracted work from another company.
2. Increase direct quote requests from local residential clients.
3. Present the business as professional, dependable, and easy to contact.
4. Give potential clients enough information to decide whether to reach out.
5. Support bilingual communication so English- and French-speaking visitors can use the site comfortably.

## User Goals

Visitors should be able to answer the following questions within a few seconds:

1. Is this a real, trustworthy local cleaning business?
2. Do they clean condos or residential homes like mine?
3. Do they serve my area in Montreal?
4. How do I contact them or request a quote?
5. Can I communicate in English or French?

## Target Audience

Primary audience:

1. Condo owners in Saint-Laurent and nearby Montreal areas.
2. Tenants looking for reliable recurring or one-time cleaning help.
3. Busy residents who want a straightforward, trustworthy cleaning service.

Secondary audience:

1. People moving in or out of condos or apartments, if that service is offered.
2. Existing referrals who want to check legitimacy before making contact.

## Website Goals

1. Create a professional first impression in under 10 seconds.
2. Make contact options obvious on every important page.
3. Keep the content structure simple enough for future updates without a full redesign.
4. Support local SEO for Montreal and Saint-Laurent cleaning searches.
5. Work exceptionally well on mobile, since many leads will come from phones.

## Non-Goals For V1

To keep the first version focused, the following are out of scope unless the business specifically needs them:

1. Online account creation.
2. Online payment processing.
3. Full self-serve booking calendar.
4. Blog or content marketing system.
5. Complex service area search tools.
6. Live chat.

## Core Pages And Purpose

### Home

Purpose:
Introduce the business, build trust quickly, explain the service area and cleaning focus, and push visitors toward a quote request or direct contact.

Must include:

1. Clear headline and short value proposition.
2. Main CTA above the fold.
3. Service summary.
4. Trust signals such as testimonials, local focus, response promise, or years of experience if confirmed.
5. Brief explanation of English/French support.

### Services

Purpose:
Explain what types of cleaning are offered, who they are for, and what a potential client should expect before asking for a quote.

Must include:

1. Service categories.
2. What is included or commonly included.
3. Types of properties served.
4. Service area reminder.
5. CTA to request a quote.

### About

Purpose:
Humanize the business and answer the trust question: "Why should I contact this company instead of another one?"

Must include:

1. Short business story.
2. Values and working style.
3. Local service focus.
4. Bilingual communication note.
5. Honest trust signals only. Do not claim anything unverified.

### Contact

Purpose:
Remove friction from lead generation and give visitors multiple ways to reach out.

Must include:

1. Quote/contact form.
2. Phone and email.
3. Preferred contact method options.
4. Preferred language field.
5. Service area summary.

### Reviews / Testimonials Section

Purpose:
Reinforce trust with real client feedback.

Recommended placement:

1. Summary section on the Home page.
2. Additional testimonials on the Services or About page.
3. A dedicated page is optional later if enough testimonials are collected.

## Key Features

1. Bilingual English/French support with a clear language switcher.
2. Mobile-first responsive layout.
3. Strong, repeated CTA for quote requests or direct contact.
4. Testimonials or reviews as trust signals.
5. Service-area messaging for Montreal and Saint-Laurent.
6. SEO-ready page metadata and structured content.
7. Maintainable content structure for future edits.
8. Fast loading and simple navigation.

## Lead Capture Requirements

The site should make it easy for a serious visitor to contact the business without forcing a long process.

Recommended form fields for V1:

1. Name
2. Phone number
3. Email
4. Neighborhood or area
5. Property type
6. Type of cleaning needed
7. Preferred language
8. Preferred contact method
9. Short message

Practical note:
If the team is more comfortable following up in English than French, the preferred language field becomes operationally important, not just a UX detail.

## Bilingual Requirement

Bilingual support is a core requirement, not a later enhancement.

Project requirements:

1. Every public page must exist in English and French.
2. The language switcher must be visible and easy to use on desktop and mobile.
3. Navigation, CTAs, forms, validation messages, metadata, and footer content must all be translated.
4. Page structure should stay aligned between languages so future updates do not drift.
5. Translation should be handled in a structured content system, not by scattering hard-coded text across components.

Content rule:
Do not rely on machine translation alone for launch copy. French pages should be reviewed by a strong French speaker before going live.

## Content And Branding Direction

### Voice And Tone

The site should feel:

1. Professional
2. Simple
3. Trustworthy
4. Local
5. Human

The copy should avoid:

1. Generic marketing hype
2. Overpromising
3. Corporate jargon
4. Luxury-brand language unless that is intentionally chosen later
5. Claims like "best in Montreal" unless supported

### Messaging Priorities

The messaging should repeatedly communicate:

1. Reliable residential and condo cleaning
2. Local service in Saint-Laurent and nearby Montreal areas
3. Easy, direct communication
4. Respectful and dependable service
5. English and French support

### Trust Content Rules

Only publish claims that can be verified.

Examples of claims to confirm before using:

1. Years in business
2. Bonded or insured status
3. Eco-friendly products
4. Satisfaction guarantee
5. Move-in/move-out specialty

### Visual Direction

The visual style should be:

1. Clean and calm rather than flashy.
2. Bright and readable, with strong contrast.
3. Friendly and local, not corporate.
4. Built around clarity and trust, not decoration.

Preferred assets:

1. Real photos of the team or real work when available.
2. Real service-area references.
3. Minimal icons used only when they improve scannability.

## Technical Goals

1. Keep the site fast, lightweight, and easy to maintain.
2. Use a structure that supports bilingual content cleanly.
3. Separate content from presentation so text updates are straightforward.
4. Make SEO, analytics, and form handling easy to add and maintain.
5. Keep the codebase simple enough for future updates by one person.
6. Avoid unnecessary dependencies and complex CMS setup in version 1.

## Suggested Tech Stack

Recommended stack for this project:

1. **Next.js** for routing, SEO control, deployment flexibility, and a strong long-term foundation.
2. **TypeScript** for safer content structures and maintainable code.
3. **Tailwind CSS** for fast, consistent UI development without heavy custom CSS overhead.
4. **next-intl** or an equivalent i18n library for bilingual routing and translation management.
5. **Zod** for validating form input and any structured content objects.
6. **Vercel** for simple deployment, previews, and low-friction hosting.
7. **Plausible** or **Google Analytics 4** for tracking quote requests and contact behavior.
8. **Formspree**, **Resend**, or a simple server-side email flow for contact form delivery in V1.

Why this stack:

1. It is modern but still practical.
2. It supports strong SEO.
3. It handles bilingual routing well.
4. It leaves room for future growth without overengineering the first release.

## SEO Goals

The SEO strategy should stay local and practical.

Primary goals:

1. Rank for local intent searches related to residential and condo cleaning in Montreal.
2. Make each page clear about service type and service area.
3. Ensure English and French pages are discoverable and not treated as messy duplicates.

SEO requirements:

1. Unique page title and meta description for each page and each language.
2. Clean URLs with consistent bilingual structure.
3. Proper `hreflang` implementation.
4. Sitemap and `robots.txt`.
5. Local business structured data.
6. Service-area content that naturally references Saint-Laurent and nearby Montreal areas.
7. Fast page speed and optimized images.
8. Internal links between key pages.

Keyword directions to support:

1. Montreal cleaning service
2. Saint-Laurent cleaning service
3. Condo cleaning Montreal
4. Residential cleaning Montreal
5. Cleaning service near Saint-Laurent

Important note:
Local SEO should be honest. Do not stuff neighborhood names or publish fake location pages.

## Accessibility Goals

The site should meet a practical WCAG AA baseline.

Requirements:

1. Semantic HTML and correct heading structure.
2. Keyboard-accessible navigation and forms.
3. Strong color contrast.
4. Visible focus states.
5. Clear form labels and error messaging.
6. Meaningful alt text for important images.
7. Correct `lang` attributes for English and French pages.
8. Readable text sizing and spacing on mobile.

## Content Needed Before Build

The following business inputs should be collected early because they affect both copy and structure:

1. Official phone number and email address to publish.
2. Exact service areas to mention.
3. Final service list.
4. Whether recurring cleaning, deep cleaning, and move-in/move-out cleaning are offered.
5. Testimonials that can be published.
6. Team or business photos, if available.
7. Hours of operation or expected response time.
8. Any trust credentials that are actually true and publishable.
9. Preferred process for handling English vs French inquiries.

## Future Improvements

Possible post-launch improvements:

1. Add a dedicated testimonials page once enough reviews exist.
2. Add FAQ content based on real customer questions.
3. Add service detail pages if SEO demand justifies them.
4. Add simple analytics dashboards for lead tracking.
5. Add a lightweight CMS or admin-friendly content workflow if updates become frequent.
6. Add photo galleries or before/after case studies if strong assets become available.
